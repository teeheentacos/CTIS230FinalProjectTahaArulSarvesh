Installing the RAM

First consult the motherboard manual and locate the correct slots to place your RAM. Most motherboards support dual channel RAM and have an alternating mounting pattern. For example if you are using 2 sticks of RAM and 4 RAM slots, odds are you would mount those sticks alternating instead of side by side. After you have found the correct slots for your RAM, locate the notch on the bottom of the RAM stick and make sure the stick is orientated so that notch lines up with the notch in the RAM slot on the motherboard. Now that you have the correct RAM slot and the correct orientation, if not already "open", "open" the RAM slot by pushing the plastic lock on the side of the slot outwards. Place the RAM stick into the slot and push down until that plastic lock "closes" and is standing upright; this might require a little force. You should also hear a click indicating your RAM is correctly installed.
