Installing the PSU

This is it, the final step of the build! The PSU is usually mounted on the bottom of the case and some cases cover it with a PSU shroud. If not already removed, remove the back plate of the case and locate where the PSU should go. There is a distinct "back" that has ports for the main power cable. Orientate the PSU so this "back" faces the hole in the case for the PSU, if not you won't be able to power your PC! There will also be screw holes on the "back" of the PSU, use the included screws to lock the PSU into place.

Below is a checklist of everything that requires power from the PSU and the connector needed.

  • The motherboard, requires 24 pin connector
  • The GPU, requirements vary from 6, 8, and 12 pins
  • The CPU, 4 or 8 pins, this connection is on the motherboard
  • Storage Drives, SATA power connection
