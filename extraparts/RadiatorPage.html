Radiators

A radiator is used in water cooling. You run water through a cooling block over the CPU or GPU, then it goes through a radiator where it pushes out the heat by air that is blown through the radiator by fans.

If you use air cooling, then you don't need a radiator. If you use a liquid cooling kit, the radiator will be part of that kit so you still don't need a radiator in this case. If you build a custom water cooling loop then you would have to buy a radiator and install it. There are different size radiators and different materials used to build radiators. The larger the radiator, the more components you will be able to cool. The materials you can use are brass-copper, aluminum, and full copper. These don't really have much impact on the cooling performance, but choosing copper will reduce corrosion over time.
