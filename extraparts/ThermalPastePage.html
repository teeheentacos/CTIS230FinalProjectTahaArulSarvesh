Thermal Paste

Thermal paste is a thermally conductive chemical compound, which is commonly used between heat sinks and heat sources such as CPUs and GPUs. The main role of thermal paste is to eliminate air gaps from the area to maximize heat dissipation, making the cooling system more efficient.

Some CPUs and heat sinks or coolers may come with thermal paste preapplied or with a bottle of thermal paste. But most of the time, with custom built and pre-built PCs, it is beneficial to remove preapplied paste and apply store bought brands, since manufacturers may cheap out on factory applied paste. The highest quality thermal pastes are from reputable brands such as Artic Silver or Noctua.

Not all thermal pastes are the same, however, they can be made up of many different ingredients. There are metal based compounds, ceramic based compounds, and silicone based compounds. These are important to consider because each different type has different properties such as thermal conductivity, density, viscosity, and conductivity. The optimal levels of each of these properties will depend on your use case. For example, a PC built for gaming would be better performing if a metal based thermal compund was used because metal has much better conductivity.
